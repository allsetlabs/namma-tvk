## School Dropout Rates (UDISE+ 2024-25)

| Level | 2023-24 | 2024-25 | Change |
|-------|---------|---------|--------|
| Primary | 0% | 2.7% | ▲ Sharp rise |
| Upper Primary | Previous low | 2.8% | ▲ 5-year high |
| Secondary | 7.7% | **8.5%** | ▲ Alarming |

- Tamil Nadu ranks **9th from the bottom** nationally at primary level
- Better than national averages at upper primary (3.5% national) and secondary (11.5% national) — but the **reversal trend is the story**
- Decade of progress reversed in one academic year

## Enrollment Decline in Government Schools

- Class 1 government school enrollment: **2.8 lakh (2023-24) → 2.7 lakh (2024-25)**
- Government-aided schools: 97,692 → 91,694
- Private schools: 5.17 lakh → 5.62 lakh (**parents fleeing public schools**)

## Education Budget Allocation

| Year | Allocation |
|------|-----------|
| 2022-23 | ₹36,895 crore |
| 2023-24 | ₹40,299 crore |
| 2024-25 | ₹40,042 crore (↓ slight drop) |
| 2025-26 | ₹46,767 crore |

- TN allocates **13.7% of total expenditure** on education (2024-25) — below aspirational 18% TVK target
- School Education department got ₹48,534 crore total but higher education only ₹6,967 crore (2023-24)

## For Website: Before vs After 5 Years

- **Before (2021):** TN was a model state — near-zero primary dropout
- **After (2026):** Secondary dropout 8.5%, reversal of decade of progress, public school enrollment declining
- **TVK Target:** 18% of budget to education (vs current 13.7%)
- Causes: Post-COVID economic pressure pushing children to labour; government failed to prevent fallout
